Description

Cheesecake is a rich and creamy dessert made primarily from cream cheese, sugar, and eggs, often set on a crumbly crust made of graham crackers or cookies. This indulgent treat can be baked or served as a no-bake version, and it comes in various flavors, including classic vanilla, chocolate, fruit, and even savory varieties. The texture of cheesecake can range from dense and creamy to light and fluffy, depending on the recipe. Popular toppings include fruit compotes, chocolate ganache, or whipped cream. Cheesecake is enjoyed worldwide, with regional variations like Italian ricotta cheesecake or Japanese cotton cheesecake. Often served at celebrations and special occasions, it has become a beloved dessert for many, combining simplicity with a luxurious taste.

Ingerdients

  • Cream Cheese - 16 oz (2 packages, softened)
  • Sugar - 1 cup
  • Graham Cracker Crust - 1 pre-made or homemade
  • Vanilla Extract - 1 tsp

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. Mix Filling: In a bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  3. Prepare Crust: If using a homemade crust, mix crushed graham crackers with melted butter and press into a 9-inch springform pan.
  4. Pour Filling: Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust, smoothing the top.
  5. Bake: Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the center is set and slightly jiggles. Let it cool, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving. Enjoy!
